Thriving on campus

Navigate add/drop.

Add/drop is the window at the start of each semester when you can change your schedule without academic penalty. Knowing how to use it is one of the most valuable things you can learn as a freshman.

Find your school's exact add/drop deadline — it varies
Most schools have a 5–12 business day add/drop window after the first day of class. After that, dropping may result in a "W" on your transcript, a partial refund, or no refund. This date is in your school's academic calendar — know it before the semester starts.
Use the first week to shop classes, not just attend them
The first week is your free trial period. Attend the first session of any class you are unsure about — even if you are not yet enrolled or are on a waitlist. Most professors allow this, and many waitlisted students get a spot by showing up in person.
Add before you drop — don't accidentally go below full-time
If you need to swap courses, add the new one first before dropping the old one. Dropping below full-time status (typically 12 credit hours) can affect your financial aid, health insurance under a parent's plan, and housing eligibility.
Email the professor to get off a waitlist
If a course you need is full, email the professor directly. Introduce yourself, explain why you need the course, and ask if they anticipate spots opening. Attend the first class regardless. Professors have discretion to add students directly in many cases.
A late withdrawal looks different than never enrolling
A "W" on your transcript is not the end of the world, but too many raise flags. One W for a genuine reason is fine. Four W's across two years tells a different story. Withdraw if you need to, but be intentional about it.
